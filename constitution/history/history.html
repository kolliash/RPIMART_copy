History

The history of the Constitution can be traced back to September 17, 1787. The founding fathers of the United States of America were entrusted with the crucial task of fixing the Articles of Confederation, the initial constitution established after the Revolutionary War in 1777. At that time, the Articles of Confederation had flaws that weakened the nation economically and led to conflicts among the states. Accordingly, a constitutional convention was convened in 1787 to address these issues. During this period, there was a strong sense of unity and purpose, as civil sessions were held to promote collaboration and improvement. Over 17 weeks, the participants worked tirelessly to devise a government system that ensured equality for all, now recognized as the checks and balances system.
